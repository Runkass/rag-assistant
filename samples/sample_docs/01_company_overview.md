# Acme Robotics — Company Overview

Acme Robotics is a fictional company used in the rag-assistant demo dataset.
It was founded in 2017 in Munich and now operates three engineering offices:
Munich (HQ), Lisbon, and a small remote-first hub in Yerevan.

The company designs warehouse-automation robots: autonomous mobile platforms
(AMRs) for shelf-to-station transport, lightweight pick-and-place arms, and a
fleet-management software stack called Acme Conductor.

## Org structure

Acme Robotics is split into three main divisions:

* **Hardware** — mechanical, electrical, and firmware teams. Roughly 60 people.
* **Software** — Conductor backend, simulation, and the customer-facing portal.
  Around 45 engineers.
* **Operations** — manufacturing, logistics, and customer success. About 30
  people, mostly in Munich.

The CTO is Anna Becker; she reports directly to the CEO, Markus Vogel.

## Key products

* **Acme AMR-100** — payload up to 100 kg, top speed 1.6 m/s, runtime 8 hours
  on a single charge.
* **Acme AMR-300** — heavy-duty platform, payload 300 kg, also 1.6 m/s, runtime
  6 hours. Built for pallet movement.
* **Acme Pick-Arm S1** — 6-DOF collaborative arm, reach 850 mm, payload 5 kg.
* **Acme Conductor** — cloud-hosted fleet manager. Routes jobs across the
  customer's robots, exposes a REST API, supports up to 200 robots per site.
